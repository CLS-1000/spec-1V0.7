# The Influence Desk — Product Concept

**Money → Votes → Foreign Agents. In one view. For the Portland metro and beyond.**

---

## The Product

A regular (initially weekly or event-driven) intelligence product that maps:

- Campaign contributions and lobbying
- Legislative voting records and sponsorships
- FARA registrations and proximity to decision-makers
- Cross-references that surface influence chains

Built on the existing cls_congressional + cls_fara pipelines + the 4-gate + anomaly system.

---

## Why It Matters

Traditional coverage treats these as separate stories.

The Influence Desk treats them as one system.

When a trade deal, land use decision, or policy shift happens, you see the money and the foreign agents in the same frame.

---

## Differentiation

- Deterministic scoring (not "we think this is connected")
- Local depth (no national outlet will ever do this level of sustained Portland metro coverage)
- Auditable (every link has a source and a gate it passed)
- Modular (easy to add new data sources or regions)

---

## Formats

- Weekly Influence Brief (PDF + X thread)
- On-demand entity reports (for paid users)
- Anomaly alerts when unusual proximity or spending patterns appear

---

## Positioning

"The Bloomberg Terminal for local political influence — except it actually shows you the foreign agent and dark money connections most dashboards ignore."

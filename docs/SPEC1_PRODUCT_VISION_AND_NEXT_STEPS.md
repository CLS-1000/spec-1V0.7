## How We Get There — Next Step → Next Step

**Next Step 1: Own the local product in public**

Make the Metro Citizens Brief the unmistakable daily reference for Portland metro power and influence.

Actions:
- Treat MCB as the primary product, not a side output.
- Give it a clean public home (dedicated site or section) with archive, search, and "why this matters" framing.
- Double down on the 06:00 PT X thread as the habit-forming distribution channel.

**Next Step 2: Make the Influence Desk visible**

Package the Congressional + FARA work as a coherent "Influence Map" product.

Actions:
- Create a regular (weekly or as-triggered) Influence Brief or dashboard that shows money → votes → FARA proximity in one view.
- Use one concrete recent example (e.g., a live trade or FARA filing) to demonstrate the unique value in a public thread or short report.

**Next Step 3: Turn the anomaly layer into a product**

The combination of cls_psyop + cls_journalist + cls_pdx1 anomaly detection is genuinely differentiated.

Actions:
- Surface "Anomaly Alerts" as a distinct, high-signal output (separate from the daily brief when something hot appears).
- Start with one clean narrative: "We detected X pattern that traditional monitoring missed."

**Next Step 4: Build the owned audience**

The current audience (researchers, journalists, advocates, small biz) is perfect. They are the exact people who will become evangelists and paying customers.

Actions:
- Launch a proper email version of MCB (the X thread is good; email is ownership).
- Create a simple "request coverage" or "suggest an entity" intake that turns users into co-producers.

**Next Step 5: Productize the modularity (the real moat)**

The fact that you can spin up sophisticated new desks (PDX1, Congressional, etc.) without breaking the core is the strongest competitive advantage.

Actions:
- Document and lightly productize the "Regional Accountability Desk" pattern.
- Use the existing PDX1 success as the case study: "We built a full bi-state legislative + finance + entity intelligence system in months because the platform already existed."

**Next Step 6: Create the first paid surface**

Once the free daily product has clear value and some audience, introduce the first paid layer.

Natural progression:
- Paid archives + advanced search
- Custom entity monitoring / alerts
- "Influence Map" deep access
- Methodology + adapter licensing for other groups

**Next Step 7: Position for the right funders and partners**

The product is already aligned with what serious local journalism and accountability funders want.

Actions:
- Use the operational reality (live daily MCB + Congressional + FARA + anomaly detection) as the proof.
- Talk to the people already funding the Oregon Journalism Project and similar efforts. The pitch is infrastructure, not another newsroom.
